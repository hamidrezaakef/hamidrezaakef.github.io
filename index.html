About

I'm a PhD student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin, advised by David Soloveichik. My research lies at the intersection of molecular programming and DNA computing, where I study the fundamental capabilities and limitations of programmable molecular systems. More broadly, I'm interested in developing mathematical frameworks for engineering biomolecular computation and exploring their potential applications in synthetic biology and RNA networks.

Before joining UT Austin, I earned my BSc from the University of Tehran, where I was recognized in Iran's National Mechanics Olympiad. Along the way, I've worked on projects spanning robotics, cognitive science, and molecular computation, and have served as a teaching assistant at both UT Austin and the University of Tehran.

Beyond research, I'm interested in entrepreneurship, translational science, psychology, hiking, and music.
